What are nebulae?

Our solar system began in a nebula about 15 billion years ago. All the matter in the universe was packed into an incredibly small, dense area. A huge explosion happened, and everything scattered in all directions. Over billions of years, gravity pulled space dust and gases together into clouds.These huge, shining clouds are called nebulae —a single cloud is called a nebula. Many stars, like our sun, were born in the clouds of the nebulae.

The largest, most violent star-forming region known lies in the Tarantula Nebula.The bright knot of stars to the left of the center contains many of the largest, hottest, and brightest stars known. Click through the slideshow to see a variety of known nebulae found in our galaxy and beyond.

The Ring Nebula (M57) is a famous planetary nebula, a glowing shell of gas ejected by a dying, sun-like star, located about 2,500 light-years away in the constellation Lyra, appearing as a doughnut or ring due to our line of sight. Its structure reveals intricate filaments, concentric arcs, and a central white dwarf star, with colors indicating different elements like red nitrogen, green oxygen, and blue helium, beautifully captured by telescopes like Hubble and James Webb. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] Key Characteristics: • Type: Planetary Nebula (dying star's outer layers). [1, 3] • Appearance: A classic doughnut or ring shape, though it's a complex 3D structure (like a rugby ball in a donut hole). [5, 6] • Location: Constellation Lyra, visible with a small telescope. [3, 7] • Distance: Approximately 2,000-2,500 light-years away. [1, 3, 8] • Composition & Color: Red (nitrogen), green (oxygen), blue (helium). [4] • Central Star: A hot, faint white dwarf, the remnant core of the original star. [5, 8] How it Forms: • A star similar to our Sun runs out of fuel, sheds its outer layers. [1, 2] • The exposed, hot core emits intense ultraviolet radiation, ionizing the expelled gas and making it glow. [4] Discovery: • Discovered in 1779 by Antoine Darquier de Pellepoix and Charles Messier, who cataloged it as M57. [3, 5, 9]
